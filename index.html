2013 was a big year for games in general but especially in the horror space. Among hits like The Last of Us, Luigi's Mansion: Dark Moon, The Walking Dead: 400 Days and Amnesia: A Machine for Pigs many would argue that the best horror game of said year was the first of the trilogy: Outlast.

The Story

Outlast is a AAA (triple-A) game released by an indie studio Red Barrel on 4th of September 2013. It was the first of its kind to feature found footage and making the camera an integral part of the gameplay. The story features a reporter Miles Upshur who receives an email by Waylon Park, a software engineer at Murkoff Corp, about the wrong doings, budget cutting and patient abuse at the company. That piques Miles' interest and is also the main motivation for him venturing into Mount Massive Asylum. Said building is a real place located in the remote mountains of Lake County, Colorado, USA. With this information Miles wants to get the 'big scoop,'' hence the usage of a camcorder. The camera can be used in two separate ways, firstly Miles can use it to record evidence of Murkoff's crimes and secondly it can be used to navigate the darkness with its 'night-vision' setting which adds to the horror atmosphere.

Review

I first became aware of Outlast at 12 years old. PewDiePie was the main reason why I became interested in the horror game genre in the first place. I remember it being overly scary however I did not have the means to play it myself and I was also too scared. Now, 11 years later, I finally worked up the courage (and got a better PC) to play it for myself. It surprisingly still holds up not only as a horror game but also as a speed-running game since there is plenty of fun glitches to exploit, which I have also tried out myself. The most common detriment of a horror game is its lacklustre story, however, Outlast delivered in having a believable story (for the most part) and interesting characters, and enemies who you could learn more about if you picked up the confidential files scattered around the asylum. I have played both the original game and the DLC: Whistleblower, and both are enjoyable. Many players, like myself, like the DLC better. It is by no means a cash-grab and that is made clear by the pricing. The first two games of the trilogy are affordable on steam for 19,50€ and 8,79€ each.

Whether you are interested in the story, speed-running or just want to play an excellent horror game for an affordable price, Outlast is sure to be a good pick.

Score: 9/10
